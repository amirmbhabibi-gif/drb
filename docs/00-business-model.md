# DaruGard – Business Model

## What DaruGard Is Not

- Not an e-commerce storefront.
- Not a pharmacy management system.
- Not a drug catalog or ERP replacement.

## What DaruGard Is

A **Trust + Liquidity + Matching + Risk Reduction layer** for pharmaceutical inventory exchange between pharmacies.

The pharmaceutical supply chain has a structural inefficiency: pharmacies routinely hold expired, near-expiry, or surplus inventory while simultaneously struggling to source drugs that are in shortage. The market to clear this mismatch exists—but trust and discoverability are missing.

DaruGard is the network that fixes this.

## Core Market Insight

Phase 1 must NOT require structured drug data. Forcing pharmacies to look up standardized drug IDs (GTIN, IRC, INN) before posting creates friction that kills early-stage liquidity. A pharmacy with 20 boxes of near-expiry amoxicillin needs to post in 30 seconds, not 5 minutes.

**Liquidity precedes correctness.** We build intelligence on top of the market, not as a prerequisite to it.

## Network Effects Model

DaruGard benefits from same-side and cross-side network effects:

- **Cross-side**: every new OFFER makes the network more valuable to pharmacies with NEEDs, and vice versa.
- **Same-side (trust)**: as more pharmacies transact successfully, the reputation layer builds credibility for all future participants.
- **Data flywheel**: as listings accumulate, DaruGard learns drug demand patterns, shortfall locations, and seasonal trends—enabling a matching and intelligence moat that competitors cannot replicate.

## Value Proposition by Stakeholder

| Stakeholder | Problem | DaruGard's Solution |
|---|---|---|
| Surplus pharmacy | Near-expiry stock becoming a loss | Post an OFFER, find a buyer in hours |
| Shortage pharmacy | Cannot source a drug through distributors | Post a NEED, get matched to surplus |
| Industry regulator | Informal gray markets, opaque pricing | Auditable exchange record, verified pharmacy identities |
| Distributor | Returns and recalls create write-offs | Early redistribution channel |

## Why Now

1. Pharmacies in emerging markets are increasingly connected but lack peer-to-peer channels.
2. Drug shortage frequency is increasing globally (post-COVID supply chain fragility).
3. Regulatory pressure on waste reduction creates institutional demand for a formal channel.
4. No dominant player exists at the B2B pharmacy exchange layer in this market.
